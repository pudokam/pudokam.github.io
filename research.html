maria.research

My research primarily involves high redshift quasars and their local environments.

I am currently using data from the Large Binocular Telescope (LBT) and the Canada-France-Hawaii Telescope (CFHT) to search for a large scale overdensity of galaxies around the z=6.3 quasar J010013.02+280225.8 as it is the most massive high-redshift quasar known and likely resides in a massive hark matter halo.

I work on the ASPIRE team and will soon be using JWST data to understand galaxy clustering through cosmic time.

Large Scale Overdensity of Lyman Break Galaxies Around z=6.3 Quasar J0100+2802
Python Astropy Corrfunc EAZY

I have built the Python architecture needed to select for Lyman Break galaxies, filter out low-redshift interlopers and non-astronomical sources, and calculate the photometric redshifts with EAZY given their photometric information along with calculating the angular autocorrelation function of the galaxies.

Stellar Angular Momentum Evolution
Python IRAF

For my undergraduate thesis, I analyzed spectroscopic rotation rates of field stars and low-mass stars in the Alpha Persei open cluster to extract information about stellar ages from spectroscopic measures alone.

Clustering Through Cosmic Time
Python JWST

I will use data from two JWST surveys, ASPIRE and FRESCO, to calculate the clustering of [OIII]+Hβ emitters on small scales for redshifts z~6.5-9. (Image from Costa et al. 2014)
